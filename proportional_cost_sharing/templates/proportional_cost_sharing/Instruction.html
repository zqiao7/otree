Instructions

In this study, you will be in a randomly formed group of {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants. Your group must decide whether or not to purchase a fictitious good, called a gadget, and exactly which members of your group are allowed to use the gadget.

For your group to purchase a gadget, your group must pay a total of {{ Constants.cost }}. To determine whether your group buys the gadget, every member of the group will simultaneously submit a message. Every participant must choose to contribute a non-negative integer.

The messages are used to determine whether your group buys the gadget. If the sum of the contributions exceeds the total cost {{ Constants.cost }}, the gadget is purchased and all participants with positive contributions are users. Each user shares a proportion (his/her own contribution over the total contribution) of the total cost. Nonuser pays nothing and don't get the access to the gadget.

If the sum of the contributions is less than the total cost {{ Constants.cost }}, the gadget is not purchased. All participants pay nothing and don't get access to the gadget.

Before making any decision, you will learn the user value of the gadget to you. The value of the gadget may differ from participant to participant and is randomly and independently drawn from {{ Constants.valuation }} for each participant. Notice that no one else in your group knows your user value and you do not know the user value of anyone else in your group.

If the gadget is not purchased, all participants are nonusers. If the gadget is purchased, those participants who contribute a positive amount are users and those who contribute 0 are nonusers.

Nonuser's payoff is always 0 since they contributes and receives nothing.

If the gadget is purchased, the user's payoff is calculated as (in points)
User's payoff = His or her private value of the gadget - Total cost of the gadget *(His or her contribution / Total contribution)

Note that the "Total contribution" is the sum of contributions from everyone and can exceed the "Total cost".
